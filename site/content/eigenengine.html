What it is

An end-to-end research environment built around a memory. The engine compiles a research program's working material — notes, sources, numerics, drafts, its author's rulings — into a conceptric: a graded graph of typed concepts that persists as the work moves. The loop runs continuously: a session reconstructs its working context from the graph, does its work, and deposits the results — findings, feedback, decisions — back into the structure before it closes. Papers, talks, this page and the demo are all generated from the graph; the graph, never a draft, is what is stored.

the falqon graph: concepts, links · conceptric_v1.json

What distinguishes it

The whole loop is inside, and every step of it is inspectable. Planning is data over the graph; writing is judged by separate mechanical review that refuses to release a failing build; every paragraph traces back through plan and graph to source. And it is a theory made operational: the synthetics working drafts — statistical lawhood under finite description — supply the mathematics, the engine is that mathematics running, and early versions of the engine generated the drafts that describe it.

The machine

One pipeline, five stages

Select a stage — it opens in place. Every count is the falqon manuscript's, generated from the engine tree at commit .

Everything begins as ordinary files: manuscript sources, numerics with their readings files, feedback ledgers, prior drafts. No worker reads the pile flat — a dispatched agent is pointed at one node of the structure and a goal, and reconstructs the context it needs from there. The dispatch layer enforces the rule on every dispatched prompt.

Dispatched workers also run at or below the orchestrating model's own tier, and an audit walks every workflow to check it, rather than trusting the author of the dispatch.

The loop closes here: review findings, the author's rulings and reader feedback land back in the same ledgers this stage reads, so the next build starts from more structure than the last one did.

Every compiled build is read by a panel of detector classes — leaked planning language, captions, apparatus, claims, house style, visuals, template, promises. Findings are typed records carrying evidence; nothing counts as closed without it, and the machinery has refused its own operator's claimed closures on exactly that ground. The loop refuses to release a build while any hard gate fails.

Two counters describe this build and they move differently: the review ledger has banked findings against the candidate in total, while the detector pass above counts still open at round — the count rises across rounds because the detector suite widens as it learns the manuscript. The aim

Where this goes

The falqon manuscript is the first object worked end to end inside the engine; the engine's own paper is queued behind it. The destination is further out: a partner intelligence grown from one person's life and work — a memory of a whole research life, structured, auditable, and running on hardware its author owns. What is on this page is that destination's working prototype: the loop already runs, the memory already persists, and everything it produces already shows its evidence.
