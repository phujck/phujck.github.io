Why Algebra

Little Learning Machine — Post 1

These five pieces accompany the first post in the Little Learning Machine series. The post argues that algebra is the correct language for any genuinely invariant description of dynamics, and that the dimensionality-nonlinearity trade-off is the central cost of realising intelligence in matter. Each visualisation makes one step of that argument interactive.

🌀
Phase Portrait — Damped Pendulum
Explore the phase space of a damped pendulum. Click anywhere to seed a trajectory and watch the system evolve — stable fixed points, unstable saddles, and the basins that separate them. The geometry of state space made interactive.

The Koopman Operator — Same Dynamics, Two Representations
The same nonlinear trajectory rendered side by side: state space on the left, observable space on the right. The Koopman operator is linear on observables regardless of the nonlinearity of the underlying flow — both panels show identical physics.

The Dimensionality–Nonlinearity Trade
A dynamical system's complexity budget is fixed. Slide between high-dimensional linear representations and low-dimensional nonlinear ones — and watch what each choice costs. The total complexity is invariant; only the allocation changes.
EDMD Open →
🔲
Constraint Carves Structure from Noise
A system without constraints is indistinguishable from noise. Add structure one constraint at a time and watch coherent patterns crystallise from an otherwise featureless background. Constraint is what it means to be a system rather than a collection of uncorrelated events.
Universality Open →
📐
Iterative Lifting — Carleman Linearisation
Advance the dictionary order D from 1 to 4. At each step a new observable axis is added and a new time-series panel appears. The y-residual collapses to zero the moment x² enters the dictionary — the nonlinear transient was always a linear combination in disguise.
