Synthetics

A living experiment in agentic orchestration and the physical architecture of finite description.

The Framework

Statistical Lawhood Under Finite Description

This is not a finished architecture. It is an active laboratory. The arguments contained here derive the thermodynamic toll of drawing a boundary and forcing a complete universe through a finite descriptive frame.

The Premise
Synthetics is the study of finite representations of lawful systems - how laws are produced, transformed, and partially lost under changes of description. A representation sorts antecedent generator structure into a synthetic decomposition: null law (absorbed background), visible cost (retained complexity), and closure residue (structured failure). Four axioms on scalar law-content uniquely force KL divergence. The universe is constrained, and friction is the mathematical mandate of survival.
The Orchestra

The Phases of Hierarchy

The overarching architecture of this project maps strictly to the formal transition of organisational loss - from catastrophic failure to rigid stability.

I

The Flat Phase

Exploratory and volatile. The loss function is catastrophically unstable. Initial probes into self-consistent loss geometries and the foundational scaffolding of complexity costs reside here.

II

The Modular Phase

Operationally viable. Bounded by an interior optimum of depth, this phase clusters functions. The exact Hamiltonian of Mean Force and structured closure failures emerge here, stabilising the description.

III

The Inertial Phase

Rigid and hardened. The protocols and the "Law of Laws" formalisms are codified. Agentic orchestration runs deterministically under the Laplace architecture, resisting perturbation.
